Local Monument Honored with New Plaza

Remembering Dr. José Maria Costa: A Life Dedicated to Tuna's Heart and Mind

Tuna News brings you an inspiring story from our community. Earlier this week, a new public square was officially dedicated in honor of Dr. José Maria Costa, a prominent figure known for his contributions to local education and cultural preservation.

The Dr. José Maria Costa Square, located at the heart of Tuna city center, was unveiled during a ceremony attended by local officials, friends, and family members of the esteemed doctor. The square features a decorative plaque with Dr. Costa’s image and a plaque that reads: "In Grateful Memory of Dr. José Maria Costa – A Life Dedicated to Our Community."

The Timeless Tune of Tuna: Portugal's Fishermen Keep Tradition Alive

Catch and Culture: Tuna Fishing in Modern Portugal

In the heart of Portugal’s rugged coast, where the Atlantic meets the land, a legacy as old as the sea itself continues to thrive. The small, picturesque fishing villages along the Alentejo and Setúbal coasts are home to a tradition that has defined the region for centuries: the art of tuna fishing. Today, however, this tradition is evolving, not just in how it’s practiced, but in its very essence.

A Tradition Born from the Depths

The story of tuna fishing in Portugal is as much a part of the nation’s cultural identity as its iconic architecture or cuisine. For generations, local fishermen have relied on their expertise to harvest these majestic fish, working closely with the rhythms of the ocean and the needs of their families. Tuna was once a staple in Portuguese diets, used for everything from sustenance to celebration.

The Big Catch: Local Fishermen Reel in Record Tuna

The Big Catch: Record Tuna Spawn Surprise in Porto Moniz

In an exciting turn of events, the tiny coastal village of Porto Moniz has recently made headlines for an unusual reason—record-sized tuna have been spotted off its shores. This unexpected twist has not only brought joy to the faces of local fishermen but also sparked curiosity among marine enthusiasts across Portugal.

Tuna fishing in Porto Moniz is a tradition that dates back centuries, with the region's waters known for their rich biodiversity and prime conditions for the elusive bluefin tuna. Recently, however, the village has witnessed something extraordinary: a series of massive tuna have been caught near the coast, with some weighing over 1,000 pounds each. Experts say this is part of a natural cycle but still an impressive feat for such a small community.
